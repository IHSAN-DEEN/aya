# aya

> In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

A command-line interface that reorients your developer workflow into a spiritual discipline. It uses the language of git‚Äîinit, commit, push, pull‚Äîto help you track the most important repository of all: your soul.


## üîÑ The Workflow

The core commands of `aya` map your daily developer habits to spiritual practices.

| Command | Concept | Function |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| `aya init` | **Niyyah** (Intention) | Sets your intention before starting work. "Actions are by intentions." |
| `aya status` | **Muhasabah** (Audit) | Checks your spiritual state. Are you mindful or heedless? |
| `aya commit` | **Deed** (Amal) | Records a good deed. Fastens it to your history. |
| `aya push` | **Dua** (Supplication) | Uploads your needs and anxieties to the Infinite Cloud. |
| `aya pull` | **Rizq** (Provision) | Asks for guidance and sustenance to be downloaded into your life. |
| `aya blame` | **Nafs** (Self-Reproach) | Confronts your own shortcomings instead of blaming others. |
| `aya diff` | **Furqan** (Criterion) | Compares your current state to your past to ensure progress. |
| `aya repo` | **Book of Deeds** | Visualizes your spiritual contributions in a browser UI. |
