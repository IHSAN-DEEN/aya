# aya

> In the name of Allah, the Most Gracious, the Most Merciful.

## The Philosophy of the Terminal

You see, we live our lives in a constant state of input and output. We type commands, we expect results. We build complex systems, structure our data, and meticulously track every change in our repositories. But in the midst of this digital noise, amidst the compilation errors and the merge conflicts, we often forget the most fundamental repository of all: the repository of our own souls.

The word *aya* in Arabic means a sign. A miraculous sign. The verses of the Quran are called *Ayat* because they are signs pointing to the Creator. But the sun is also an *aya*. The moon is an *aya*. And perhaps, if we look closely enough, even the code we write can be an *aya*—a sign that points us back to the One who taught man what he knew not.

This tool, `aya`, is not merely a utility. It is a reminder. It is an attempt to bring the sacred into the profane, to turn the command line—a place of work—into a place of worship. It reorients the developer's workflow from a purely functional pursuit to a spiritual journey. It uses the language you know—git, status, push, pull—to teach you the language you need: the language of the heart.

## The Command Line Interface as a Spiritual Discipline

When you run a command here, you are not just executing code. You are executing an intention. You are aligning your will, for a brief moment, with the Will that governs the stars.

### Core Workflow

*   **aya init**
    *   *The Renewal of Intention (Niyyah)*. Before any project, before any line of code, there must be a purpose. Just as a repository cannot exist without initialization, a deed cannot rise to the heavens without a sincere intention. Use this to orient your heart before your hands touch the keyboard.

*   **aya status**
    *   *The Self-Audit (Muhasabah)*. In git, we check for modified files. In life, we must check the state of our hearts. Are we in a state of heedlessness (Ghaflah) or remembrance (Dhikr)? This command forces you to pause and assess your spiritual standing.

*   **aya commit**
    *   *The Recorded Deed*. "And We have fastened every man's deeds to his neck." Every action is a commit hash in the history of your life. Make sure your commit messages—your intentions—are clear, for they will be read on a Day when no revert is possible.

*   **aya push**
    *   *The Supplication (Dua)*. You have needs. You have anxieties. You have bugs you cannot fix. Push them up. Transfer your burdens from your limited local environment to the Infinite Cloud of Divine Mercy. He listens. He answers.

*   **aya pull**
    *   *The Provision (Rizq)*. We think we generate success. We do not. We merely pull from what has already been written for us. Ask for guidance, for sustenance, for knowledge, and pull it down into your life.

*   **aya blame**
    *   *The Accusing Soul (Nafs Al-Lawwama)*. When things break, we look for who is responsible. But the believer looks inward first. "Whatever good strikes you is from Allah, and whatever evil strikes you is from yourself." Use this to confront your own shortcomings.

*   **aya diff**
    *   *The Criterion (Al-Furqan)*. Distinguish between Truth (Haqq) and Falsehood (Batil). See the difference between who you were yesterday and who you are today. Are you progressing, or are you regressing?

*   **aya repo**
    *   *The Book of Deeds*. A visualization of your efforts. On that Day, your book will be placed open. This command gives you a glimpse of that record now, while you still have time to edit the manuscript.

### The Toolkit of the Believer

*   **aya journal**: *The Daily Reflection*. Write down your thoughts, your gratitudes, your struggles. Writing is a form of shackles for fleeting wisdom.
*   **aya tafsir**: *The Exegesis*. Do not just read the code of the universe; understand its documentation. Delve into the meanings of the Revelation.
*   **aya memorize**: *The Preservation (Hifz)*. Commit the Words of Allah to the memory of your heart, not just the memory of your machine.
*   **aya qibla**: *The Orientation*. Wherever you are, turn your face towards the Sacred House. Realign your physical direction to match your spiritual direction.
*   **aya adhan**: *The Call*. When the time comes, stop. Success (Falah) is not in the next function; it is in the prayer.
*   **aya prayers**: *The Obligation (Salah)*. The five pillars that hold up the structure of your day.
*   **aya wudu**: *The Purification*. Wash away the sins of the limbs just as you clear the cache of your browser.
*   **aya tasbih**: *The Glorification*. Keep your tongue moist with the remembrance of the Perfection of your Lord.
*   **aya names**: *The Beautiful Names (Asma ul Husna)*. Know Him to love Him.
*   **aya dua**: *The Weapon of the Believer*. A collection of supplications for every state of the human experience.
*   **aya adab**: *The Etiquette*. Knowledge without manners is like a fire without wood—it burns out quickly or destroys what is around it.
*   **aya seek**: *The Quest for Knowledge*. Seeking knowledge is an obligation upon every Muslim.
*   **aya nazm**: *The Poem*. Beauty in expression. The Quran is the ultimate poetry, but our hearts also need soft words to melt.
*   **aya parable**: *The Metaphor*. Allah sets forth parables for mankind so that they may reflect.
*   **aya history**: *The Legacy*. You stand on the shoulders of giants. Know those who came before you so you know where you are going.
*   **aya hero**: *The Examples*. The Companions, the Scholars, the Righteous. They are the standard.
*   **aya scene**: *The Visualization*. Imagine the scenes of the Hereafter as if you see them with your eyes.
*   **aya lens**: *The Perspective*. Shift your view from the temporary to the permanent.
*   **aya sound**: *The Recitation*. Let the Quran be the background music of your life.
*   **aya mirror**: *The Reflection*. The believer is a mirror to his brother. What do you see when you look at yourself?
*   **aya hijri**: *The Sacred Time*. Step out of the Gregorian construct and into the calendar that marks the migration of the Prophet (pbuh).
*   **aya fast**: *The Discipline (Sawm)*. Restrain the body to feed the soul.
*   **aya zakat**: *The Purification of Wealth*. It does not belong to you. You are merely its custodian. Purify it by giving it back.
*   **aya sunnah**: *The Way*. The path of the Beloved (pbuh). Follow his footsteps to reach his destination.
*   **aya sleep**: *The Minor Death*. Rest is worship if the intention is right.
*   **aya anatomy**: *The Design*. Reflect on the complexity of your own creation. "And in yourselves, do you not see?"
*   **aya nature**: *The Cosmos*. The heavens and the earth are filled with signs for those who think.
*   **aya journey**: *The Path*. We are all travelers.
*   **aya invest**: *The Transaction*. "Shall I guide you to a trade that will save you from a painful punishment?"
*   **aya sabr**: *The Patience*. Perseverance is not passive; it is active endurance.
*   **aya shukr**: *The Gratitude*. If you are grateful, He will surely increase you.
*   **aya friday**: *The Day of Gathering (Jumu'ah)*. The best day the sun has risen upon.

## Installation

To bring this tool into your environment, you must first ensure you have the necessary runtime (Node.js). Then, execute:

```bash
npm install -g islamic-cli
```

Or, if you wish to contribute to the source:

```bash
git clone https://github.com/shajedulkarim/islamic-cli.git
cd islamic-cli
npm install
npm run build
npm link
```
